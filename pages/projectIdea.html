Planner App for People with Intellectual Disabilities

Overview

A project I have been interested in developing recently is a planner/routine app for people with intellectual disabilities. With the huge variety of planners available on the app store there is a surprising lack of simple, customisable apps for those who may need help adjusting to changes in daily activities or understanding what each day is going to look like. The aim of this project is to create an app that caters to both carers/parents and the person with the disability, being customisable enough for both children and adults with varying degrees of disability.

Motivation

Approximately 240,000 people care for someone with a mental disability in Australia. One of those people is my sister, who has recently become a disability support worker. Talking with her I realised how limited communicating routines and plans with clients is in her workplace, simply using a pinboard and verbal communication. There is a lack of personal planning apps designed for those who struggle with change and moving schedules such as people with autism. Creating a free, easy to use app available on both iOS and Android that is able to help people with their day to day activities has the potential to be an important and impactful project to undertake.

Description

The app has two main target users: carers/parents and people with intellectual disabilities. Each group of users have very different needs and a routine app will need to have separate and distinct profiles made available.

For users who have intellectual disabilities the main focus is to create a streamlined and interactive experience. The aim is to have a personal day planner that can be accessed and used without the need for supervision. To achieve this only one day is visible for the disabled person, showing the day's activities on one scrolling page. No changes can be made from this page, it is designed to only show the schedule in a fun and ebay way. At the top of the page is the day, followed by blocks of time containing any number of interactive elements set by the parent/carer. Elements can include short written names/descriptions, pictures that can be tapped to fill the screen, short videos, playable sound recordings, and a range of customisable colours and vibration options. For those who live in disability care houses, names and pictures of carers can be assigned to each block of time to allow an easy to access schedule of who is on duty. By making every element customisable and savable a wide spectrum of needs can be met, catering to people who all respond differently to various stimuli. Some standard pictures and videos such as doctors offices or public parks will be made available, but the main purpose is to create your own personalised entries, making the daily schedules familiar and comfortable.

For carers/parents a separate login is needed to access all the settings and customisable elements needed to prepare the schedule. A full calendar is available to view and any future date can be edited as needed. Blocks of time can be set to a variety of intervals from 10 minutes to 1 hour. Each block is able to hold as many text, picture, video, or sound elements as needed, inserted with an easy menu selection or search function. Taking pictures, videos, and recording sounds is all done within the app and it is encouraged to get the client involved in this process as much as possible. To make repetitive activities easier to manage, a group of elements can be saved together as a single element to assign to a block of time. How the disabled person views their planner will also have a wide range of settings. Background colour/image, font type, font size, font colour, clock visibility, and day/date settings can all be changed to suit each individual.

Tools and Technologies

Developing apps for Android and iOS require different tools for each platform. Android uses the Java language and will need the Java Development Kit , Android Studio , and Android USB Drivers if you want to run your app on a physical device.

iOS uses the Swift language and will need XCode (available in the app store).

Graphic design software may also be needed such as Photoshop, Sketch, or GIMP.

A version control system such as GitHub is also recommended.

Skills Required

Developing mobile apps is very accessible, all the needed software can be downloaded for free (although XCode is available only on Mac computers, there are work around options available for Windows users). Testing apps is also very easy as development tools come with emulators for a huge variety of phones and tablets with the option to use a physical device available. The most difficult aspect is learning the programming languages needed. Java is a common language and there are many fantastic resources available online for free such as www.w3schools.com or www.geeksforgeeks.org. Swift is only used for Apple, however is a clean and concise language and has a few good free resources available online such as www.codeacademy.com.

Outcome

A customisable, easy to use planner for people with intellectual disabilities can make a positive impact on both the person who is disabled and their carer or parent. For many who are disabled, change and unfamiliar places and things can be a huge obstacle to face. By providing an app designed with them in mind change may become a less stressful event with accessible information displayed in a fun and positive way. By allowing as much customisation as possible, the app can be changed to suit both children and adults with varying degrees of disability.
